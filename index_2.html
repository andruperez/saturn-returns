The Return of Saturn

June 27, 2026  ·  4–8 pm

Amato Studio  ·  Arlington Heights, Los Angeles

Dear        ,

My Saturn has returned, and with it a desire to be surrounded by art and community.

For my 30th, I've decided to throw an art show. The theme revolves around Saturn Return, the astrological rite of passage when Saturn completes its first orbit back to where it was at your birth —roughly ages 27–30. It's associated with confronting mortality, identity, and authenticity; the stripping away of borrowed personas; and the collapse of structures built unconsciously.

I don't want to do this show alone. My hope is to have my friends' artwork fill the walls, each owning their own narrative and interpretation of this cosmic inner confrontation.

I want to formally ask your involvement in this project. I'd love to see your work on the walls of the gallery. Whether it's artwork you've already worked on or a new idea, I'm open to anything.

The date & tentative time: June 27, 2026 — 4–8pm
The venue: Amato Studio, in the Arlington Heights community of LA. It's a 1920s bank that has been converted into an event space and production studio, with 20-foot-high ceilings, so you're welcome to go big.
Tentative title: The Return of Saturn
My project's tone: Romantic era with digital twists. Shadows of the psyche coming to light. A realization that "joy and woe are woven fine." Natural Fibers. My own battles with creativity and being an artist. The inner turmoil of identity and grief through an abstract lens.

I will be working with cyanotypes, batik and an art installation. This is still in development, but this is where I'm at as I'm writing this letter. I'm trying not to overthink it or be held back by limiting beliefs. I just want to make work that says what my words can't. I want to put myself out there, stay present, and trust the process of making.

If you are interested, please reach out as soon as possible! I'm here if you want to brainstorm and need any more direction.

Regardless, consider this an invitation to the show—I'd love to see your face.

with love,

Andru
